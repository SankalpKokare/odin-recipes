Masala Dosa

Discription

Masala dosa is a popular South Indian dish made with a fermented lentil and rice batter that is cooked on a hot griddle to form a thin pancake. The pancake is then filled with a spicy potato curry and served with chutneys and sambar.

The name "masala dosa" comes from the two main ingredients in the dish: the dosa (which is a type of pancake) and the masala (which is the spicy potato curry). The dosa is made by soaking lentils and rice in water overnight, then grinding them together with a little water to form a batter. The batter is then fermented for a few hours, which gives it a sour flavor.

Here are some of the key ingredients in masala dosa:

  • Lentils
  • Rice
  • Potatoes
  • Spices
  • Chutneys
  • Sambar

Here are some of the steps involved in making masala dosa:

  1. Soak the lentils and rice overnight.
  2. Grind the lentils and rice together to form a batter.
  3. Ferment the batter for a few hours.
  4. Cook the potatoes in a spiced gravy.
  5. Spread the masala on the dosa.
  6. Roll up the dosa.
  7. Serve with chutneys and sambar.
